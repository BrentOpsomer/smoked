The Catcher in the Rye is a novel by J. D. Salinger, first published in 1951. It's a coming-of-age story that follows the protagonist, Holden Caulfield, as he grapples with alienation and disillusionment in the aftermath of his expulsion from prep school. The book is known for its frank portrayal of adolescent angst and its critique of conformity and adult hypocrisy.

To Kill a Mockingbird is a novel by Harper Lee, published in 1960. It's set in the fictional town of Maycomb, Alabama, and tells the story of a young girl, Scout Finch, as she learns about racial injustice and prejudice in the Deep South during the 1930s. The book is a classic of American literature and has been praised for its themes of courage, empathy, and social justice.

1984 is a dystopian novel by George Orwell, published in 1949. It's set in a future totalitarian society where individuality is suppressed and government control is absolute. The story follows the protagonist, Winston Smith, as he rebels against the oppressive regime and falls in love with a fellow dissident. The book is a warning about the dangers of authoritarianism and the importance of preserving freedom and democracy.
